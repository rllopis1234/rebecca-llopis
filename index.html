About

Rebecca Llopis

Born in Geneva, raised between Cannes and London, and now based in Los Angeles — where I'm pursuing an undergraduate degree at USC.

I am an actor at heart, a creative thinker, entrepreneur, and innovator. My work sits at the intersection of performance, production, and philosophy — driven by a belief that the most powerful stories are those brave enough to explore the unpredictable, multifaceted nature of being human.

From directing theatre productions in London to producing short films at USC's School of Cinematic Arts, to writing and photographing in my own time — I move fluidly across disciplines because I believe the best work emerges from artists who refuse to be contained by a single form.

I'm currently seeking deeper exposure on the business and production side of film, connecting artists across industries and developing projects that demand to be told.

02

Theatre · Edinburgh Fringe Festival · August 2022

How To Be Lost

Crafted and staged a performance called "How To Be Lost". As a company of twelve we brought together verbatim accounts of misplaced paths and accidental adventures and combined physical theatre, dance and puppetry. Weeks of rehearsal taught me the groundworks of devising a piece of theatre and how to perform in front of a large audience 4 days consecutively.

Zen Buddhism Maymester

Conducted ethnographic research across Tokyo, Fukui, Kyoto, and Saitama on the Sōtō school of Zen Buddhism, integrating practitioner interviews, participant observation, temple-based fieldwork, and sustained Zazen practice. Completed immersive study at Eiheiji Monastery and a residential retreat at Shokakuji Temple. Received distinction for a capstone thesis analyzing non-dualism and the influence of Western binary epistemologies on Zen thought.
